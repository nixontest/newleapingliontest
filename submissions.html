Calling All Writers!

Do you want to express your unique style or story through words? Do you have a working manuscript? Do you have dreams for it to be published? You should pitch it to Leaping Lion Books!

We're looking for inspiring and interesting manuscript submissions. Your submission is reviewed by Mike O'Connor, the course director of the Book Publishing Practicum. If accepted for publication, it will be handed over to practicum students to be, among other tasks, edited, marketed, designed and then published by Leaping Lion Books. You can submit a fiction or non-fiction manuscript, but be ready to take it through to maturity with students passionate about learning the intricacies of the book publishing industry.

Things to keep in mind before submitting your manuscript

1. Unsolicited manuscripts are welcome.
2. Authors must be Canadian.
3. Submissions must be sent as PDF or Microsoft Word doc files.

4. Put your name and address on the manuscript (not just the cover letter), as manuscripts can and do get lost.

5. Do not send original art or photos.

6. Attach with your submission a letter of enquiry that includes an outline or summary, bio, publication credits (if applicable) and a sample of the work.

7. We hold a track record for debuting many Canadian authors. Currently, Leaping Lion Books is working with Professor Arthur Haberman on his first mystery novel, Wild Justice. Although a former publication is an asset, it is not a requirement.

8. Contact us with a query first: Send us a short pitch before sending along your full manuscript.

9. Check out our catalogue for a taste of what we have published in the past. Our former publications include full-length fiction and non-fiction such as Nic Labriola’s short story collection, Francis and the Animals (2013), Donna Jean MacKinnon’s non-fiction book, Newsgirls (2017), Bruce Jay Friedman’s collected plays, 3.1 Plays (2012) and many others.
